# Adobe - PM Interview Intel
Last updated: 2026-03-24
Sources: Glassdoor, levels.fyi, Blind
Confidence: High

## Company Overview
- Stage: FAANG+ / Public (ADBE)
- PM team size estimate: 1,000-1,500 PMs across Creative Cloud, Document Cloud, Experience Cloud, and Adobe Express
- PM comp range: Senior PM $200K-$340K TC | Principal PM $280K-$450K TC | Director $400K-$600K+ TC
- Culture notes: Creative-focused culture with strong design DNA. PMs bridge creative professionals and enterprise marketers. Work-life balance is generally good. Emphasis on craft and quality.

## Interview Format
- Total rounds: 4-5
- Timeline: 3-5 weeks
- Round 0: Recruiter screen - Recruiter - 30 min
- Round 1: Hiring Manager screen - HM - 45-60 min
- Round 2: Product sense / design - PM - 45-60 min
- Round 3: Behavioral / leadership - PM or cross-functional - 45-60 min
- Round 4: VP or Director conversation - 30-45 min

## Question Bank
### Behavioral
- "Tell me about a time you shipped a product for a creative audience." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Describe how you balanced professional and consumer user needs." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Tell me about a time you used data to change product direction." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)

### Product Sense / Design
- "How would you improve Photoshop for beginners?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Design an AI-powered feature for Adobe Express." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "How would you make Acrobat more useful for knowledge workers?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Design a collaborative feature for Illustrator." (frequency: occasional, source: Glassdoor)

### Execution / Metrics
- "What metrics matter for Creative Cloud subscription retention?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "How would you measure the success of Adobe Firefly (generative AI)?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Creative Cloud free trial conversion dropped. Diagnose the issue." (frequency: occasional, source: Glassdoor)

### Technical
- "How does generative AI work at a high level? How would you apply it to creative tools?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "What are the technical challenges of real-time collaboration in design tools?" (frequency: occasional, source: Glassdoor)

## What They Screen For
- Creative empathy: understanding of how creative professionals work (source: Glassdoor)
- Product craft: attention to user experience and design quality (source: Glassdoor)
- AI vision: how generative AI transforms creative workflows (source: Glassdoor)
- Data fluency: ability to define and track SaaS metrics (source: Glassdoor)

## Tips
- Adobe is investing heavily in generative AI (Firefly). Show understanding of how AI can augment (not replace) creative professionals. (source: Glassdoor)
- Know the difference between Creative Cloud (prosumer), Document Cloud (enterprise), and Experience Cloud (marketing). PM roles differ significantly. (source: Blind)
- Adobe competes with Canva, Figma (which Adobe tried to acquire), and emerging AI tools. Show competitive awareness. (source: Glassdoor)
