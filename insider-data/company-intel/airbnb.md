# Airbnb - PM Interview Intel
Last updated: 2026-03-24
Sources: Glassdoor, levels.fyi, igotanoffer, Blind
Confidence: High

## Company Overview
- Stage: FAANG+ / Public (ABNB)
- PM team size estimate: 200-400 PMs across Hosting, Guest Experience, Trust & Safety, Payments, and Experiences
- PM comp range: L4 (PM) $200K-$310K TC | L5 (Senior PM) $300K-$480K TC | L6 (Staff PM) $450K-$650K+ TC
- Culture notes: Design-led culture founded by designers (Brian Chesky, Joe Gebbia). Unique approach where PMs work in a "functional model" -- Chesky reorganized in 2023 so PMs report to a central PM org rather than being embedded in teams. Strong emphasis on craft, belonging, and "11-star experiences."

## Interview Format
- Total rounds: 5-6
- Timeline: 4-6 weeks from first screen to offer
- Round 0: Recruiter screen - Recruiter - 30 min
- Round 1: PM phone screen - PM - 45 min (product sense)
- Round 2: Onsite Round 1 - PM - 45 min (Product Design / Sense)
- Round 3: Onsite Round 2 - PM - 45 min (Execution / Metrics)
- Round 4: Onsite Round 3 - Cross-functional - 45 min (Behavioral / Culture)
- Round 5: Hiring Manager or Director - 30-45 min (strategy / fit)

## Question Bank
### Behavioral
- "Tell me about a time you championed user experience over short-term metrics." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Describe how you've built trust with cross-functional teams." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "What does belonging mean to you?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor -- Airbnb-specific)
- "Tell me about a time you shipped something you were truly proud of." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)

### Product Sense / Design
- "How would you improve the Airbnb search experience?" (frequency: very common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Design a feature to help first-time hosts get started." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "How would you improve trust and safety on Airbnb?" (frequency: common, source: igotanoffer)
- "Design an Airbnb feature for long-term stays / digital nomads." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "How would you design Airbnb Experiences 2.0?" (frequency: occasional, source: Glassdoor)

### Execution / Metrics
- "What metrics would you use to measure the health of the Airbnb marketplace?" (frequency: very common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Airbnb bookings dropped 10% in a market. Diagnose the problem." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "How would you measure the success of a new trust feature?" (frequency: common, source: igotanoffer)

### Technical
- "How does Airbnb's search ranking algorithm work?" (frequency: occasional, source: Glassdoor)
- "What technical challenges exist in building a global payments system?" (frequency: occasional, source: Glassdoor)

## What They Screen For
- Design sensibility: appreciation for craft and user experience (source: Glassdoor)
- Marketplace thinking: understanding supply (hosts) and demand (guests) dynamics (source: igotanoffer)
- Mission alignment: genuine connection to Airbnb's mission of belonging (source: Glassdoor)
- Cross-functional collaboration: ability to work in Airbnb's functional model (source: Blind)
- Product craft: ability to sweat the details on product quality (source: Glassdoor)

## Tips
- Brian Chesky has a strong vision for how PM should work at Airbnb. Read his public talks about the functional model and product development. (source: Blind)
- Airbnb is design-led. When answering product questions, lead with user experience and emotional resonance, not just metrics. (source: Glassdoor)
- Always consider both sides of the marketplace: hosts and guests. Show how your solution benefits both. (source: igotanoffer)
- The "11-star experience" framework is core to how Airbnb thinks about product. Start with the current experience, then envision the 11-star version, then work backward to what's feasible. (source: Glassdoor)
- Airbnb's functional model means PMs have less autonomy than at some companies. Show that you can thrive in a more centralized product org. (source: Blind)
