# Amazon - PM Interview Intel
Last updated: 2026-03-24
Sources: Glassdoor, levels.fyi, igotanoffer, Blind, Amazon Leadership Principles documentation
Confidence: High

## Company Overview
- Stage: FAANG (public - AMZN)
- PM team size estimate: 8,000+ PMs across Retail, AWS, Alexa, Prime Video, Ads, and more
- PM comp range: L5 $150K-$250K TC | L6 $220K-$380K TC | L7 $350K-$550K TC | L8 $500K-$900K+ TC
- Culture notes: Deeply customer-obsessed, built on 16 Leadership Principles (LPs). PMs write narratives (6-pagers, PRFAQs) instead of slides. High ownership expectations; PMs function as "CEOs of their product."

## Interview Format
- Total rounds: 5-6 (including phone screens)
- Timeline: 3-6 weeks from first screen to offer
- Round 0: Recruiter screen - Recruiter - 30 min
- Round 1: Phone screen - Hiring Manager - 45-60 min (behavioral + product)
- Round 2: Onsite "Loop" Round 1 - PM - 60 min (Leadership Principles behavioral)
- Round 3: Onsite "Loop" Round 2 - PM - 60 min (Leadership Principles behavioral)
- Round 4: Onsite "Loop" Round 3 - PM or Eng - 60 min (Product / Technical)
- Round 5: Onsite "Loop" Round 4 - "Bar Raiser" - 60 min (Leadership Principles deep-dive)
- Note: The Bar Raiser is an independent interviewer from outside the team who has veto power

## Question Bank
### Behavioral (Amazon's core -- 70%+ of interview is behavioral)
- "Tell me about a time you took ownership of a difficult problem." (frequency: very common, source: Glassdoor -- maps to Ownership)
- "Tell me about a time when you had to earn trust with a difficult stakeholder." (frequency: very common, source: Glassdoor -- maps to Earn Trust)
- "Describe a time when you dove deep into data to solve a problem." (frequency: very common, source: Glassdoor -- maps to Dive Deep)
- "Tell me about a time you had to deliver results under tight constraints." (frequency: very common, source: Glassdoor -- maps to Deliver Results)
- "Tell me about a time you challenged the status quo." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor -- maps to Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit)
- "Describe a time you simplified a complex problem for customers." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor -- maps to Invent and Simplify)
- "Tell me about a time you raised the bar for your team." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor -- maps to Insist on the Highest Standards)
- "Give me an example of a big bet you took that didn't pay off." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor -- maps to Bias for Action)
- "Tell me about a time when you put the customer first even when it was hard." (frequency: very common, source: Glassdoor -- maps to Customer Obsession)
- "Describe a time you mentored or developed someone on your team." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor -- maps to Hire and Develop the Best)

## What They Screen For
- Leadership Principle alignment: every answer is evaluated against specific LPs (source: Glassdoor)
- Customer obsession: genuinely starts with the customer and works backward (source: Glassdoor)
- Ownership: takes accountability, doesn't say "that's not my job" (source: Blind)
- Data-driven decision making: can quantify impact and use metrics (source: igotanoffer)
- Bias for action: prefers calculated risk-taking over analysis paralysis (source: Glassdoor)
- Narrative ability: Amazon uses written docs, so clear thinking is prized (source: Blind)

## Tips
- Prepare 2-3 stories for EACH of the 16 Leadership Principles. Amazon interviews are LP interviews. (source: igotanoffer)
- Use the STAR format (Situation, Task, Action, Result) with heavy emphasis on YOUR specific actions and measurable results. (source: Glassdoor)
- The Bar Raiser has veto power and will go deep on LPs you haven't been asked about yet. Expect curveball LP questions. (source: Blind)
- Amazon values specificity. Replace "we did X" with "I did X, which resulted in Y% improvement." (source: Glassdoor)
- Know the PRFAQ format: start with the press release (customer benefit) then the FAQ (stakeholder questions). Even if you don't write one in the interview, the thinking model matters. (source: Aakash Gupta)
- Amazon PM roles vary dramatically by org (Retail vs. AWS vs. Alexa). Research the specific team. (source: Blind)
- Comp at Amazon is heavily back-loaded with RSUs that vest 5%/15%/40%/40% over 4 years. Negotiate the sign-on bonus. (source: levels.fyi)
