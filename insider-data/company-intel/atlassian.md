# Atlassian - PM Interview Intel
Last updated: 2026-03-24
Sources: Glassdoor, levels.fyi, Blind
Confidence: High

## Company Overview
- Stage: Public (TEAM)
- PM team size estimate: 400-600 PMs across Jira, Confluence, Trello, Bitbucket, Loom, and Rovo AI
- PM comp range: P4 $200K-$320K TC | P5 $280K-$440K TC | P6 $400K-$600K+ TC
- Culture notes: Australian-founded (Sydney HQ). Values: "Open company, no BS," "Build with heart and balance," "Don't f*** the customer," "Play as a team," "Be the change you seek." PLG motion. Strong engineering culture.

## Interview Format
- Total rounds: 4-5
- Timeline: 3-6 weeks
- Round 0: Recruiter screen - 30 min
- Round 1: Hiring Manager screen - 45 min
- Round 2: Product craft case - 60 min
- Round 3: Values interview - 45-60 min
- Round 4: Leadership / VP - 30-45 min

## Question Bank
### Product Sense / Design
- "How would you improve Jira for non-technical users?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Design a feature to improve Confluence's collaboration." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "How would you integrate AI into Atlassian's products?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)

### Execution / Metrics
- "What metrics matter for a productivity platform?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Jira project creation is up but active usage is flat. Diagnose." (frequency: occasional, source: Glassdoor)

## What They Screen For
- Values alignment: Atlassian takes its values very seriously (source: Glassdoor)
- Product-led growth understanding (source: Glassdoor)
- B2B/enterprise product sense (source: Glassdoor)
- User empathy for both technical and non-technical users (source: Glassdoor)

## Tips
- Prepare for the values interview with specific examples mapped to Atlassian's values. (source: Glassdoor)
- Know the full product portfolio and how they connect (Jira + Confluence + Trello + Bitbucket). (source: Glassdoor)
- Atlassian's PLG motion is core. Understand how they grow bottom-up in organizations. (source: Blind)
- Rovo (AI) is Atlassian's big AI bet. Know it. (source: Glassdoor)
