# Databricks - PM Interview Intel
Last updated: 2026-03-24
Sources: Glassdoor, levels.fyi, Blind
Confidence: High

## Company Overview
- Stage: Late-stage private (valued at $62B+ as of 2025)
- PM team size estimate: 150-250 PMs across Data Engineering, ML Platform, SQL Analytics, Unity Catalog, and AI/BI
- PM comp range: Senior PM $280K-$450K TC | Staff PM $400K-$600K TC | Director $550K-$800K+ TC
- Culture notes: Developer and data-focused culture. Founded by the creators of Apache Spark. Open-source DNA with strong enterprise business. Ali Ghodsi (CEO) is ambitious and execution-focused. Strong emphasis on technical depth and data platform expertise.

## Interview Format
- Total rounds: 5-6
- Timeline: 3-5 weeks
- Round 0: Recruiter screen - 30 min
- Round 1: Hiring Manager screen - 45-60 min
- Round 2: Product case - PM - 60 min
- Round 3: Technical depth - Engineer or PM - 60 min
- Round 4: Behavioral / leadership - PM - 45-60 min
- Round 5: VP or Director - 30-45 min

## Question Bank
### Behavioral
- "Tell me about a time you built a product for data engineers or data scientists." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Describe how you worked with a technical user base." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Why Databricks? What excites you about the data/AI platform space?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)

### Product Sense / Design
- "How would you improve the Databricks notebook experience?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Design a feature to simplify ML model deployment on Databricks." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "How would you design the data governance experience in Unity Catalog?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Design a product to make Databricks accessible to business analysts." (frequency: occasional, source: Glassdoor)

### Execution / Metrics
- "How would you measure the success of a new Databricks feature?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "What metrics matter for a data platform?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Databricks usage dropped among a customer cohort. Investigate." (frequency: occasional, source: Glassdoor)

### Technical
- "Explain the lakehouse architecture." (frequency: very common, source: Glassdoor)
- "What are the differences between Databricks and Snowflake?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "How does Apache Spark work at a high level?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "What are the challenges of building a unified data and AI platform?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)

## What They Screen For
- Data platform expertise: deep understanding of data engineering, analytics, and ML workflows (source: Glassdoor)
- Technical credibility: ability to discuss architecture and infrastructure (source: Glassdoor)
- Developer empathy: building for highly technical users (source: Glassdoor)
- Enterprise B2B sense: understanding enterprise sales, adoption, and expansion (source: Glassdoor)

## Tips
- Know the lakehouse architecture. It's Databricks' core value proposition. (source: Glassdoor)
- Understand the competitive landscape: Snowflake, AWS (Redshift/Glue), Google BigQuery, Microsoft Fabric. (source: Glassdoor)
- Databricks is making a big push into AI/ML (MLflow, Model Serving, Mosaic). Show understanding of the AI platform opportunity. (source: Blind)
- The technical bar is high. Be prepared to discuss data architecture, SQL, Spark, and ML pipelines. (source: Glassdoor)
- IPO is expected soon. Equity is a significant part of comp. (source: levels.fyi)
