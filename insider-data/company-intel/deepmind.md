# DeepMind (Google DeepMind) - PM Interview Intel
Last updated: 2026-03-24
Sources: Aakash Gupta's AI PM Interview Guide, Glassdoor, levels.fyi, Blind
Confidence: Medium-High

## Company Overview
- Stage: Division of Alphabet/Google (merged Google Brain + DeepMind in 2023)
- PM team size estimate: 100-200 PMs across Gemini, AI research products, Health, and Science
- PM comp range: L5 $300K-$480K TC | L6 $420K-$680K TC | L7 $600K-$1M+ TC (Google levels)
- Culture notes: Research-first culture. PMs serve research, not the other way around. Based in London (HQ) and Mountain View. Highly academic environment; many PhDs. Working on the frontier of AGI within Google's ecosystem.

## Interview Format
- Total rounds: 5-6 (follows modified Google interview process)
- Timeline: 4-8 weeks (Google hiring committee adds time)
- Round 0: Recruiter screen - Recruiter - 30 min
- Round 1: Phone screen - PM or Researcher - 45 min
- Round 2-5: Onsite rounds (similar to Google) - PMs, Researchers, Engineers - 45 min each
- Final: Google hiring committee review
- Note: Expect deeper technical AI/ML questions than standard Google PM interviews

## Question Bank
### Behavioral
- "Tell me about a time you worked with researchers to ship a product." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Describe a time you translated complex research into a product opportunity." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "How do you handle disagreements with researchers about product direction?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)

### Product Sense / Design
- "Design a product using Gemini for healthcare diagnostics." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "How would you improve Gemini's multimodal capabilities for consumers?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Design a product that uses AI for scientific discovery." (frequency: occasional, source: Glassdoor)
- "How would you productize a breakthrough AI research result?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)

### Execution / Metrics
- "How would you measure the success of Gemini?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "How do you evaluate AI model quality for a product?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Design an evaluation framework for a new AI capability." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)

### Technical
- "Explain how transformer models work." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "What are the differences between Gemini and GPT architectures?" (frequency: occasional, source: Glassdoor)
- "How does multimodal AI work?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "What are the challenges of deploying large AI models in production?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)

## What They Screen For
- AI/ML depth: strong technical understanding of modern AI (source: Glassdoor)
- Research empathy: ability to work with and earn trust of researchers (source: Glassdoor)
- Product-research bridge: translating capabilities into user value (source: Glassdoor)
- Googleyness: same as Google core values (source: Glassdoor)
- Long-term thinking: comfort with research timelines, not just sprint cycles (source: Blind)

## Tips
- DeepMind PMs operate at the intersection of research and product. You must be comfortable with uncertainty about timelines and outcomes. (source: Glassdoor)
- Know DeepMind's research breakthroughs: AlphaFold, AlphaGo, Gemini, and their science/health work. (source: Glassdoor)
- London is the primary hub. Be prepared to discuss working in a global, research-first environment. (source: Blind)
- The interview process goes through Google's hiring committee. Same tips as Google apply (structured answers, committee-friendly). (source: Glassdoor)
- Comp follows Google levels but DeepMind roles may come with research-tier equity or bonuses. (source: levels.fyi)
