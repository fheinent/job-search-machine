# DoorDash - PM Interview Intel
Last updated: 2026-03-24
Sources: Glassdoor, levels.fyi, igotanoffer, Blind
Confidence: High

## Company Overview
- Stage: Public (DASH)
- PM team size estimate: 300-500 PMs across Consumer, Merchant, Dasher (Driver), DashPass, Ads, and Wolt (international)
- PM comp range: L4 (PM) $190K-$290K TC | L5 (Senior PM) $280K-$430K TC | L6 (Staff PM) $400K-$600K+ TC
- Culture notes: Highly execution-oriented, scrappy despite public company size. CEO Tony Xu sets a demanding pace. All employees (including PMs) do "WeDash" -- monthly delivery shifts. Strong emphasis on local commerce and operational excellence.

## Interview Format
- Total rounds: 5-6
- Timeline: 3-5 weeks
- Round 0: Recruiter screen - Recruiter - 30 min
- Round 1: PM phone screen - PM - 45 min (product sense or analytics)
- Round 2: Onsite Round 1 - PM - 45 min (Product Sense)
- Round 3: Onsite Round 2 - PM - 45 min (Analytical / Metrics)
- Round 4: Onsite Round 3 - PM - 45 min (Behavioral / Strategy)
- Round 5: Cross-functional (Eng or Ops) - 45 min

## Question Bank
### Behavioral
- "Tell me about a time you were scrappy in solving a problem." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Describe a time you went above and beyond for a customer/user." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Tell me about a time you operated with a bias for action." (frequency: common, source: Blind)
- "Why DoorDash? Have you done a WeDash?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)

### Product Sense / Design
- "How would you improve the DoorDash restaurant discovery experience?" (frequency: very common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Design a feature to reduce delivery times." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "How would you improve the Dasher (driver) experience?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Design a product for DoorDash to enter grocery delivery." (frequency: common, source: igotanoffer)
- "How would you improve DashPass retention?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)

### Execution / Metrics
- "What metrics would you track for DoorDash's three-sided marketplace?" (frequency: very common, source: Glassdoor)
- "DoorDash order volume dropped 8% in a market. Diagnose it." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "How would you measure the success of DoorDash's ads platform?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Design a metric framework for merchant success on DoorDash." (frequency: occasional, source: igotanoffer)

### Technical
- "How does DoorDash optimize delivery routing?" (frequency: occasional, source: Glassdoor)
- "What are the technical challenges of a three-sided marketplace?" (frequency: occasional, source: Glassdoor)

## What They Screen For
- Marketplace thinking: three-sided marketplace (consumer, merchant, dasher) dynamics (source: Glassdoor)
- Scrappiness and execution speed: DoorDash values operators (source: Blind)
- Analytical excellence: highly data-driven culture (source: Glassdoor)
- Customer obsession: genuine care about user experience across all three sides (source: Glassdoor)
- WeDash mindset: willingness to understand ground-level operations (source: Glassdoor)

## Tips
- DoorDash has three sides to its marketplace: consumers, merchants, and dashers. Always consider all three in your answers. (source: igotanoffer)
- Do a delivery before your interview. DoorDash takes WeDash seriously, and firsthand experience will make your answers much stronger. (source: Glassdoor)
- DoorDash is expanding beyond food delivery into grocery, convenience, retail, and ads. Show you understand the broader local commerce vision. (source: Glassdoor)
- Tony Xu's leadership style and DoorDash's cultural values are important. Research them. (source: Blind)
- The analytics round is considered the hardest. Practice metrics trees and root cause analysis extensively. (source: Glassdoor)
