# Figma - PM Interview Intel
Last updated: 2026-03-24
Sources: Glassdoor, levels.fyi, Blind
Confidence: Medium-High

## Company Overview
- Stage: Late-stage private (valued at ~$12.5B after failed Adobe acquisition)
- PM team size estimate: 60-100 PMs across Design, FigJam, Dev Mode, and Enterprise
- PM comp range: L4 $250K-$380K TC | L5 $350K-$530K TC | L6 $480K-$700K+ TC
- Culture notes: Design-first product culture. Collaborative, community-driven. "Make design accessible to everyone." PMs are design-literate and user-obsessed. SF-based with growing remote.

## Interview Format
- Total rounds: 5-6
- Timeline: 3-5 weeks
- Round 0: Recruiter screen - 30 min
- Round 1: Hiring Manager screen - 45-60 min
- Round 2: Product case - 60 min (design-oriented)
- Round 3: Analytical - 45-60 min
- Round 4: Cross-functional (Eng or Design) - 45 min
- Round 5: Leadership - 30-45 min

## Question Bank
### Product Sense / Design
- "How would you improve Figma's collaboration features?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Design a feature to help developers work better with Figma." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "How would you expand Figma beyond design to other creative workflows?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Design Figma's AI features." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)

### Execution / Metrics
- "What metrics matter for a design tool?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Figma's free-to-paid conversion dropped. What do you investigate?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)

### Technical
- "How does real-time collaboration work technically?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "What are the challenges of building a browser-based design tool?" (frequency: occasional, source: Glassdoor)

## What They Screen For
- Design literacy: understand the design process and designer needs (source: Glassdoor)
- Product-led growth instinct: Figma grows bottom-up (source: Glassdoor)
- Community awareness: Figma's community is its moat (source: Glassdoor)
- Collaboration mindset (source: Glassdoor)

## Tips
- USE Figma before interviewing. Build something. Know the product deeply. (source: Glassdoor)
- Figma competes with Adobe XD, Sketch, and emerging AI design tools. Know the landscape. (source: Glassdoor)
- Product-led growth is central. Understand how Figma grows virally within organizations. (source: Blind)
- AI integration in design is a major focus. Have ideas about how AI augments design workflows. (source: Glassdoor)
