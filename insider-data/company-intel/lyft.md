# Lyft - PM Interview Intel
Last updated: 2026-03-24
Sources: Glassdoor, levels.fyi, Blind
Confidence: High

## Company Overview
- Stage: Public (LYFT)
- PM team size estimate: 150-300 PMs across Rides, Bikes/Scooters, Driver Experience, Autonomous, and Ads
- PM comp range: T5 (PM) $170K-$260K TC | T6 (Senior PM) $250K-$380K TC | T7 (Staff PM) $350K-$500K+ TC
- Culture notes: More collaborative and values-driven than Uber. Known for better driver relations. Smaller team means PMs have broader scope. Focus on North American ridesharing market.

## Interview Format
- Total rounds: 4-5
- Timeline: 3-5 weeks
- Round 0: Recruiter screen - Recruiter - 30 min
- Round 1: PM phone screen - PM or HM - 45 min (product sense)
- Round 2: Onsite Round 1 - PM - 45 min (Product Sense / Design)
- Round 3: Onsite Round 2 - PM - 45 min (Analytical / Metrics)
- Round 4: Onsite Round 3 - Cross-functional - 45 min (Behavioral / Leadership)

## Question Bank
### Behavioral
- "Tell me about a time you had to balance competing priorities with limited resources." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Describe a time you improved a process or product using data." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Why Lyft over Uber?" (frequency: very common, source: Glassdoor)

### Product Sense / Design
- "How would you improve the Lyft rider pickup experience?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Design a feature to improve driver earnings transparency." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "How would you design Lyft's subscription product?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)

### Execution / Metrics
- "What metrics would you track for Lyft's marketplace health?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Lyft ride ETAs are longer than competitors. How do you investigate?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "How would you measure success for Lyft bikes and scooters?" (frequency: occasional, source: Glassdoor)

### Technical
- "How does ride matching work in a marketplace?" (frequency: occasional, source: Glassdoor)
- "What are the technical challenges of ETA prediction?" (frequency: occasional, source: Glassdoor)

## What They Screen For
- Marketplace thinking: supply/demand dynamics for transportation (source: Glassdoor)
- Analytical rigor: data-driven decision-making (source: Glassdoor)
- Values alignment: community, safety, driver relations (source: Glassdoor)
- Resourcefulness: doing more with less (smaller team than Uber) (source: Blind)

## Tips
- Understand how Lyft differentiates from Uber (community focus, driver-friendly, North America focus). (source: Glassdoor)
- Lyft is smaller, so PMs own broader scope. Show you can handle ambiguity and wear multiple hats. (source: Blind)
- The media/ads business is growing and is a strategic PM area. (source: Glassdoor)
- Lyft's autonomous vehicle partnerships (Waymo, etc.) are another important area to understand. (source: Glassdoor)
