# Meta - PM Interview Intel
Last updated: 2026-03-24
Sources: Glassdoor, levels.fyi, igotanoffer, Blind, Product Alliance
Confidence: High

## Company Overview
- Stage: FAANG (public - META)
- PM team size estimate: 3,000+ PMs across Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, Messenger, Reality Labs, and Threads
- PM comp range: IC3 $170K-$260K TC | IC4 $250K-$380K TC | IC5 $350K-$550K TC | IC6 $500K-$800K TC | IC7 $700K-$1.2M+ TC
- Culture notes: Move fast, extremely execution-oriented. PMs are expected to be highly data-driven and comfortable with rapid experimentation. Strong emphasis on impact and measurable outcomes over process.

## Interview Format
- Total rounds: 4-5 (including recruiter screen)
- Timeline: 3-6 weeks from first screen to offer
- Round 0: Recruiter phone screen - Recruiter - 30 min
- Round 1: Phone screen - PM - 45 min (Product Sense)
- Round 2: Onsite Round 1 - PM - 45 min (Product Sense / Design)
- Round 3: Onsite Round 2 - PM - 45 min (Execution / Metrics)
- Round 4: Onsite Round 3 - PM or EM - 45 min (Behavioral / Leadership)
- Note: Meta has three core interview dimensions: Product Sense, Execution, and Leadership/Drive

## What They Screen For
- Execution mindset: strong bias to action, can ship at high velocity (source: Glassdoor)
- Data fluency: can define metrics, interpret results, and course-correct with data (source: igotanoffer)
- Product craft: deep sense for what makes a great consumer product (source: Blind)
- Impact orientation: can articulate how their work moved the needle (source: Glassdoor)
- Leadership/Drive: Meta-specific dimension -- they want people who operate like owners (source: Glassdoor)

## Tips
- Meta's interview has three explicit dimensions: Product Sense, Execution, and Leadership/Drive. Each round maps to one. Know which dimension you're being tested on. (source: igotanoffer)
- For Product Sense, always start with the mission of the specific product (Facebook = connect, Instagram = visual expression, WhatsApp = private messaging). (source: Glassdoor)
- Meta loves the "goals, signals, metrics" framework for execution questions. Define the goal first, then signals of success, then measurable metrics. (source: Blind)
- Be specific about numbers -- if you say engagement increased, say by how much. Meta is very metrics-oriented. (source: Glassdoor)
- For behavioral rounds, use the "Leadership/Drive" framing: show ownership, bias to action, and comfort with ambiguity. (source: igotanoffer)
- The recruiter screen matters more at Meta than most places. Come prepared with a crisp "why Meta" and "biggest impact" story. (source: Blind)
