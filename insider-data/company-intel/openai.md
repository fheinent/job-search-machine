# OpenAI - PM Interview Intel
Last updated: 2026-03-24
Sources: Aakash Gupta's AI PM Interview Guide, Glassdoor, levels.fyi, Blind
Confidence: High

## Company Overview
- Stage: Late-stage private (valued at $150B+ as of early 2026)
- PM team size estimate: 100-200 PMs across ChatGPT, API Platform, Enterprise, Safety, and Research
- PM comp range: L4 $300K-$500K TC | L5 $450K-$750K TC | L6 $700K-$1.2M+ TC (significant equity component)
- Culture notes: Mission-driven ("safe and beneficial AGI"). Moves extremely fast with small, elite teams. PMs must have strong AI/ML understanding. Flat hierarchy with direct access to leadership. High autonomy and high expectations.

## Interview Format
- Total rounds: 5-7
- Timeline: 3-6 weeks (can be faster for strong candidates)
- Round 0: Recruiter screen - Recruiter - 30 min
- Round 1: Hiring Manager screen - HM - 45-60 min (product sense + AI fluency)
- Round 2: Product deep-dive - PM - 60 min (product design with AI constraints)
- Round 3: Technical / AI - Engineer or Research PM - 60 min (technical depth)
- Round 4: Analytical / Execution - PM - 45-60 min (metrics and strategy)
- Round 5: Behavioral / Culture - PM or cross-functional - 45-60 min
- Round 6 (sometimes): Senior leadership (VP or C-level) - 30-45 min

## Question Bank
### Behavioral
- "Tell me about a time you shipped a product in extreme ambiguity." (frequency: very common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Describe how you've worked with researchers or ML engineers." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Why OpenAI? What's your view on AGI safety?" (frequency: very common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Tell me about a time you had to make a decision that balanced user benefit against potential risk." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "How do you stay current on AI developments?" (frequency: common, source: Blind)

### Product Sense / Design
- "How would you improve ChatGPT for enterprise users?" (frequency: very common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Design a new ChatGPT feature for education." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "How would you design the developer experience for the OpenAI API?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Design a product that makes GPT models useful for healthcare." (frequency: occasional, source: Glassdoor)
- "How would you improve ChatGPT's memory and personalization?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Design a safety feature that reduces harmful outputs without degrading usefulness." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)

### Execution / Metrics
- "What metrics would you track for ChatGPT?" (frequency: very common, source: Glassdoor)
- "ChatGPT daily active users dropped 10%. What would you investigate?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "How would you measure the success of a new API feature?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "How would you balance free tier growth against paid conversion?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)

### Technical
- "Explain how large language models work at a high level." (frequency: very common, source: Glassdoor)
- "What is RLHF and why does it matter for product?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "What are the trade-offs between model size, latency, and cost?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "How does fine-tuning work and when would a customer use it vs. prompt engineering?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "What are hallucinations and how would you mitigate them as a PM?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Explain the difference between GPT-4, GPT-4 Turbo, and GPT-4o." (frequency: occasional, source: Glassdoor)

## What They Screen For
- AI/ML fluency: must understand LLMs, training, inference, fine-tuning, and evaluation (source: Aakash Gupta)
- Product velocity: ability to ship fast in a rapidly evolving space (source: Glassdoor)
- Safety awareness: genuine understanding of AI risks and responsible deployment (source: Glassdoor)
- Technical depth: ability to work directly with researchers and ML engineers (source: Blind)
- Mission alignment: belief in OpenAI's mission of safe AGI (source: Glassdoor)
- Ambiguity tolerance: comfortable working without established playbooks (source: Glassdoor)

## Tips
- OpenAI is the hottest PM job in tech right now. Competition is intense. Have a strong AI-native product thesis. (source: Blind)
- You MUST understand LLMs at a technical level. Know transformers, attention, RLHF, fine-tuning, context windows, and inference costs. (source: Aakash Gupta)
- Have a thoughtful view on AI safety. "I think we should go fast" is not sufficient. Show nuanced thinking about safety-utility trade-offs. (source: Glassdoor)
- Be prepared to discuss specific use cases where you've used OpenAI's products and what you'd improve. (source: Glassdoor)
- OpenAI moves incredibly fast. Show evidence of thriving in high-velocity environments. (source: Blind)
- Compensation is very high, with significant equity upside. Negotiate based on equity terms (secondary sale opportunities, etc.). (source: levels.fyi)
- Know the competitive landscape: Anthropic, Google DeepMind, Meta AI, Mistral, Cohere. (source: Glassdoor)
