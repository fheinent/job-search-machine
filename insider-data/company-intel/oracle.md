# Oracle - PM Interview Intel
Last updated: 2026-03-24
Sources: Glassdoor, levels.fyi, Blind
Confidence: Medium

## Company Overview
- Stage: FAANG+ / Public (ORCL)
- PM team size estimate: 2,000+ PMs across Oracle Cloud (OCI), Database, ERP (Fusion), NetSuite, Health, and Cerner
- PM comp range: IC3 $170K-$280K TC | IC4 $240K-$380K TC | IC5 $350K-$500K+ TC
- Culture notes: Enterprise-focused, competitive culture. Larry Ellison's influence remains strong. Significant investment in cloud infrastructure (OCI) to compete with AWS/Azure. Large company with varied sub-cultures across business units.

## Interview Format
- Total rounds: 4-5
- Timeline: 3-6 weeks
- Round 0: Recruiter screen - Recruiter - 30 min
- Round 1: Hiring Manager screen - HM - 45-60 min
- Round 2: Product / technical case - PM or Eng - 45-60 min
- Round 3: Behavioral / leadership - PM or VP - 45-60 min
- Round 4: Senior leadership (VP+) - 30-45 min

## Question Bank
### Behavioral
- "Tell me about your experience with enterprise products." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Describe a time you managed competing priorities across stakeholders." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Tell me about a time you drove adoption of a new product." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)

### Product Sense / Design
- "How would you improve the Oracle Cloud Console experience?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Design a migration tool for AWS customers moving to OCI." (frequency: occasional, source: Glassdoor)
- "How would you improve NetSuite for mid-market companies?" (frequency: occasional, source: Glassdoor)

### Execution / Metrics
- "What metrics would you track for OCI adoption?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "How would you measure success of an ERP product?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)

### Technical
- "Explain database architecture at a high level." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "What are the challenges of cloud migration for enterprises?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)

## What They Screen For
- Enterprise product expertise: understanding of B2B, SaaS, and enterprise sales (source: Glassdoor)
- Technical credibility: especially for cloud/database/infrastructure PM roles (source: Glassdoor)
- Competitive drive: Oracle has a famously competitive culture (source: Blind)
- Customer orientation: ability to understand complex enterprise workflows (source: Glassdoor)

## Tips
- Oracle is making a massive bet on OCI (Oracle Cloud Infrastructure). Cloud PM roles are the most in-demand. (source: Glassdoor)
- Oracle's health business (Cerner acquisition) is a growing PM area with unique domain requirements. (source: Glassdoor)
- Be prepared to discuss how Oracle competes with AWS, Azure, and GCP. (source: Blind)
- Compensation has improved significantly as Oracle competes for cloud talent. (source: levels.fyi)

## Compensation Data
