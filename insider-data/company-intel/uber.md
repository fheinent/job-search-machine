# Uber - PM Interview Intel
Last updated: 2026-03-24
Sources: Glassdoor, levels.fyi, igotanoffer, Blind
Confidence: High

## Company Overview
- Stage: FAANG+ / Public (UBER)
- PM team size estimate: 800-1,200 PMs across Mobility (Rides), Delivery (Eats), Freight, Advertising, and Platform
- PM comp range: L4 (PM) $180K-$280K TC | L5 (Senior PM) $280K-$430K TC | L5b (Staff PM) $380K-$550K TC | L6 (Director) $500K-$750K+ TC
- Culture notes: Fast-paced, metrics-obsessed marketplace culture. Under Dara Khosrowshahi, shifted toward responsible growth. PMs own P&L for their areas. Strong emphasis on two-sided marketplace thinking and operational excellence.

## Interview Format
- Total rounds: 5-6
- Timeline: 3-5 weeks from first screen to offer
- Round 0: Recruiter screen - Recruiter - 30 min
- Round 1: PM phone screen - PM - 45 min (product sense)
- Round 2: Onsite Round 1 - PM - 45 min (Product Sense / Design)
- Round 3: Onsite Round 2 - PM - 45 min (Analytical / Execution)
- Round 4: Onsite Round 3 - PM or Eng - 45 min (Behavioral / Leadership)
- Round 5: Onsite Round 4 - Cross-functional - 45 min (Strategy or Technical)

## Question Bank
### Behavioral
- "Tell me about a time you had to make a trade-off between user experience and business metrics." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Describe a time you operated in ambiguity with multiple stakeholders." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Tell me about a time you improved an operational process." (frequency: common, source: Blind)

### Product Sense / Design
- "How would you improve the Uber rider experience?" (frequency: very common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Design a feature to reduce Uber Eats delivery times." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "How would you design Uber for a new market (e.g., healthcare transportation)?" (frequency: common, source: igotanoffer)
- "Design a loyalty program for Uber." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "How would you improve driver retention?" (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)

### Execution / Metrics
- "What metrics would you track for Uber Eats?" (frequency: very common, source: Glassdoor)
- "Uber ride completion rate dropped 5% in a city. Diagnose the problem." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "How would you set up an A/B test for a new pricing feature?" (frequency: common, source: igotanoffer)
- "Define success metrics for a new Uber product (e.g., Uber Reserve)." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)

### Technical
- "How does Uber's matching algorithm work?" (frequency: occasional, source: Glassdoor)
- "Explain dynamic pricing (surge) and the trade-offs involved." (frequency: common, source: Glassdoor)
- "How would you design the system architecture for real-time ETA prediction?" (frequency: occasional, source: Blind)

## What They Screen For
- Marketplace thinking: understanding supply/demand dynamics and two-sided platforms (source: Glassdoor)
- Analytical rigor: ability to use data and metrics to drive decisions (source: igotanoffer)
- Operational awareness: understanding how product decisions impact operations (source: Glassdoor)
- User empathy for both sides: riders/eaters AND drivers/couriers (source: Glassdoor)
- Execution speed: ability to ship and iterate quickly (source: Blind)

## Tips
- Think about both sides of the marketplace. Every product decision at Uber affects supply (drivers) and demand (riders). (source: igotanoffer)
- Uber PMs are expected to be extremely analytical. Practice metrics trees and root cause analysis. (source: Glassdoor)
- Understand unit economics: CAC, LTV, take rate, contribution margin. (source: Blind)
- Know the competitive landscape: Lyft, DoorDash, Grab, Ola, DiDi. Be ready to discuss competitive strategy. (source: Glassdoor)
- Uber's ads business is growing rapidly and is a hot PM area. (source: Glassdoor)
