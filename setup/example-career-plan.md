## Addressing Your Weaknesses / Gaps

### Gap 1: Limited Big Tech Brand

**What the gap is:**
- You've never worked at Google, Meta, Apple, Amazon
- Perception: "Unproven at mega-scale"

**How to address:**
- **In resume:** Lead with scale metrics (300M+ inferred requests, 99.95% SLA across 150+ customers) — comparable to early-stage Google Compute Engine
- **In interviews:** "I've built infrastructure that scaled from 100 requests/day to 12K daily from early-stage startup. I'm seeking a role where I can scale that thinking to 100M+ daily requests at a larger company."
- **In materials:** Reference Google / AWS papers as context; reference your own work as counterpoint ("Our approach to X was inspired by Google's Z paper")

**Proof points:**
- Companies using your infrastructure (if Databricks uses AcmeAI, that's proof of enterprise credibility)
- Technical talks / blog posts (thought leadership on infrastructure)
- Open source contributions (if any; shows technical credibility)

### Gap 2: Haven't Led a Team of Managers

**What the gap is:**
- You've managed 2-3 PMs, not led a PM organization
- Perception: "Not ready for broader org leadership"

**How to address:**
- **In resume:** Mention mentoring and career development (PMs you've hired, coached into higher levels)
- **In interviews:** "I'm ready to lead a small PM organization (3-5 PMs). Here's how I'd approach it: [framework]. I've learned from strong managers and studied anti-patterns at larger orgs."
- **In materials:** Be clear about trajectory: "IC at this level, open to Group PM role after 2-3 more years at Principal"

**Proof points:**
- Have you influenced other PMs' careers? (mentoring, feedback, scope expansion)
- Have you built trust across orgs? (consensus-building, cross-functional respect)
- Do you understand your own leadership style? (Show self-awareness)

### Gap 3: Limited Sales / GTM Exposure

**What the gap is:**
- Your background is B2D (developer/platform); no sales-led GTM
- Perception: "Doesn't understand enterprise sales"

**How to address:**
- **Be honest:** "My experience is developer-first. I understand activation, expansion, and retention at scale. Enterprise go-to-market is new territory; here's how I'd approach learning it..."
- **In interviews:** "Tell me about your biggest challenge in GTM. What would you want me to focus on in the first 30 days?" (Shows curiosity, not defensiveness)
- **Pro tip:** If applying to GTM-heavy role, interview 2-3 sales leaders beforehand. Show you've done homework.

**Proof points:**
- Fundamentals transfer: pricing model trade-offs, customer acquisition cost, customer LTV, churn (all work in enterprise)
- Enterprise context: You've interfaced with enterprise customers at Amadeus; you understand procurement, security, compliance
- Reference enterprise dynamics: "I've worked with 500+ enterprise partners at Amadeus; I understand the multi-stakeholder buying process"
