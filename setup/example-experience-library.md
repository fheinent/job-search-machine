## Section 4: STAR Stories (Your Differentiators)

### Story 1: "From 280ms to 180ms: The Inference Latency Battle"

*Situation:* At AcmeAI, our flagship customers (3 enterprise accounts generating 60% of revenue) were complaining about model serving latency. Their production systems required <200ms P50 latency, but we were consistently hitting 280ms. The issue wasn't clear: was it model weight loading? Network hops? GPU memory fragmentation?

*Task:* I was asked to own the product strategy and stakeholder coordination to fix this, working with our infrastructure and data science teams.

*Action:* I proposed a three-pronged approach: (1) I worked with data science to profile actual inference calls (not synthetic benchmarks), discovering 40% of latency was in deserialization, not GPU compute. (2) I coordinated with infrastructure eng to redesign the serving architecture—we implemented model weight pre-loading and created a GPU allocation scheduler to reduce context switching. (3) I pushed back on sales' request for more features; instead, I made latency the Q3 OKR, blocking new feature work for 8 weeks.

*Result:* We reduced P50 latency from 280ms to 180ms (35% improvement) and increased throughput 4x. All 3 enterprise customers renewed, and we unblocked 5 new deals. The latency improvement became our top sales differentiator that year—we signed 12K daily requests YoY.

---

### Story 2: "GraphQL Adoption: From Zero to 180 Partners in 18 Months"

*Situation:* At Amadeus, we had a sprawling SOAP API serving 500+ legacy travel partners. But modern partners (startups, new OTAs) complained: integration took 4 weeks and required XML expertise. We were losing deals to competitors with simpler APIs. Leadership asked: should we invest in a GraphQL API?

*Task:* I was tasked with defining the product strategy, roadmap, and go-to-market for a new GraphQL tier.

*Action:* Instead of designing in a vacuum, I (1) interviewed 20 partners about pain points (not just "what do you want?"), (2) ran a 2-week spike with eng to validate feasibility, (3) proposed a phased rollout: beta with 10 partners, then public launch. I also (4) designed the SDK strategy simultaneously—automated code generation from OpenAPI specs meant partners could generate client libraries in seconds, not days.

*Result:* GraphQL launched publicly in Q2 2020. Within 18 months, 180 partners had migrated. Integration time dropped from 4 weeks to 1 week. 40% of new partners preferred GraphQL. The initiative generated €12M+ new ARR and became the anchor for Amadeus' API modernization strategy.

---

### Story 3: "The Claims Processing Overhaul: From 8 Steps to 3"

*Situation:* At Wefox, our claims filing funnel was broken. Customers started claims but dropped off: only 58% of initiated claims got submitted. And when they did submit, our ops team faced a 12-day manual review process. We were losing customers and burning ops resources.

*Task:* I was asked to redesign the claims filing UX and backend workflow to reduce friction and improve throughput.

*Action:* (1) I conducted 15 customer interviews and watched 20 claim sessions to understand pain points (not surveys). Insight: customers didn't understand what information was "required" vs. "nice to have." (2) I worked with design and backend eng to collapse the 8-step form into 3 mandatory steps + an optional advanced details section. (3) I added mobile-first design—customers filed claims from accident scenes, so we optimized for phone. (4) On the backend, I coordinated with data science to ship ML fraud detection; if claims passed the model confidence threshold, they auto-approved. (5) Implemented SMS status updates so customers didn't wonder what was happening.

*Result:* Mobile adoption grew from 12% to 61% of claims filed. Completion rate improved from 58% to 89%. Resolution time dropped from 12 days to 3 days average. NPS on claims improved +12 points. The workflow became a template for other markets (Germany, France, Italy).

---

### Story 4: "Killing a Feature to Save Infrastructure Costs"

*Situation:* At AcmeAI, our team had built an advanced feature: "model versioning with automatic A/B test routing." It was technically sophisticated but almost no customers used it (2 of 150). Meanwhile, it consumed 25% of our GPU infrastructure costs monthly.

*Task:* I was asked to evaluate whether we should continue investing in this feature or sunset it.

*Action:* Rather than just look at usage metrics, I (1) interviewed the 2 customers using it (they could achieve the same outcome with manual deployments, just less conveniently), (2) calculated the true cost: €18K/month in GPU, plus 1 FTE in eng maintenance. (3) Proposed a sunset: 90-day notice, we'd provide migration guidance, but we'd allocate freed engineering capacity to model serving performance (which customers actually cared about). (4) Presented the cost-benefit to leadership: "We pay €216K/year to serve 2 customers when they could achieve 95% of the outcome manually. Those resources can instead reduce latency for all 150 customers."

*Result:* The feature was deprecated. 100% of affected customers migrated smoothly (they didn't actually need the automation). We redirected that engineering capacity to the latency work, which drove major revenue growth. This decision became a template for "ruthless prioritization" in the company culture.

## Notes

- **This example is realistic but stylized.** Real experience libraries will vary (maybe you have fewer roles, or deeper domain expertise). Use this as a template, not a straitjacket.
- **Metrics matter most.** The difference between "scaled claims processing" and "shipped ML fraud detection, reducing false rejection rate from 3.2% to 1.1%" is massive. Be specific.
- **STAR stories are your interview ammunition.** Memorize 5–7 of these (1.5 min each). Mix them (1 about technical collaboration, 1 about difficult trade-off, 1 about data-driven decision, etc.).
- **Update this every quarter.** As you get interview feedback, add new stories or metrics that interviewers care about.
