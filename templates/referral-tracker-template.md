# Referral Tracker Template

Track every person who refers you into a company. A referral is way more effective than cold applications, and they usually know of upcoming positions before they're posted. This helps you:
1. Thank them properly (don't forget)
2. Follow up with them (let them know you moved forward)
3. Spot patterns (who in your network is most helpful)

---

## How to Use This

Copy this table into a spreadsheet or keep it in a markdown file.

Update it **every time:**
- You ask someone for a referral
- They agree to refer you
- You complete an interview
- You get an offer (or rejection)

**Referral status definitions:**
- **Not started:** Haven't identified a connection yet
- **Connection identified:** Found someone at the company but haven't asked
- **Request sent:** Sent the referral ask; waiting for response
- **Agreed:** They said yes; referral not yet submitted
- **Submitted (ATS only):** They submitted through the ATS referral portal
- **Strong referral (HM pinged):** They messaged the hiring manager directly on Slack/email -- this is the gold standard
- **Declined:** They said no (note why; adjust approach for next person)
- **No response:** Sent request 5+ days ago with no reply (morning briefing flags these)

## Referral Request Playbook (Quick Reference)

**Message 1 - Initial Ask:**
Generated by `/referral-request`. Send within 24 hours of applying or before applying.

**Message 2 - Strong Referral Push (after they agree):**
"Thanks so much! One more ask -- could you also ping the hiring manager ([Name] if you have them) directly? An ATS referral is great, but a direct message from you makes it 5x more likely to get attention."

**Message 3 - No Response Nudge (5+ days after initial ask):**
Keep it short and low-pressure. One bump. If no response after the bump, move to another contact.

**When they decline:**
- Thank them genuinely
- Ask: "Is there anyone else on the team you'd suggest I connect with?"
- Note the reason in the tracker (too new, don't know the HM, policy) -- it helps calibrate future asks

**When they agree but don't follow through (7+ days):**
- One gentle nudge: "Hey [Name], just checking if you were able to submit the referral. No worries if the timing doesn't work."
- If still nothing after the nudge, move to another contact. Do not send a third message.

## Action Items

### Before Each Referral

- [ ] Have you researched who at this company is in your network?
- [ ] Do you have their direct email / LinkedIn profile?
- [ ] Did you craft a personalized message (not generic)?
- [ ] Do you know what role you're asking about?

### After They Agree to Refer

- [ ] Send them a thank you message (same day)
- [ ] Tell them when you hear back
- [ ] Update this tracker

### After Each Interview

- [ ] Update tracker with interview date + outcome
- [ ] If rejected: thank them anyway (they tried, you still appreciate it)
- [ ] If advanced: let them know (good news)

### If You Get an Offer

- [ ] Send a heartfelt thank you to your referrer
- [ ] Let them know the outcome (you accepted / you're deciding)
- [ ] Consider a small gift or public credit if you share the news

### If You Get Rejected

- [ ] Still thank them (they used their credibility on you)
- [ ] Don't blame them or make excuses
- [ ] Ask if they have feedback: "If you hear anything, I'd love to learn what didn't land"

---

## Patterns to Watch

After 2-3 weeks, review your tracker and look for:

- **"Warm intros convert more than cold apps"** → Spend more time on referrals, less on cold applying
- **"Referrals from [type of person] always convert"** → Focus referral asks on that segment (e.g., ex-Stripe PMs, or people in growth roles)
- **"[Company] never responds to referrals"** → Maybe their hiring process is broken; move on
- **"I'm thanking 80% of people who help me"** → Good practice. Aim for 95%+

## Spreadsheet Setup (If Using Google Sheets)

1. Create a Google Sheet
2. Add columns
3. Sort by Company (so you can see all your activity at one company)
4. Color-code status (green = offer, yellow = pending, red = rejected)
5. Connect it with Claude
6. Share with [optional: accountability partner / mentor] to stay honest

---

## Success Metric

**Goal:** 3+ referrals per week in active job search (week 2-8)

Why 3+? 
- Many won't convert to interviews
- Of those that do, some will reject
- You need volume + referral quality to get to offers

If you're asking fewer than 3 people per week → you should be asking more.

---

## One Last Thing

**Public acknowledgment matters.** When you get an offer through a referral:
- Post on LinkedIn: "Thanks to [Name] for referring me into [Company]. Great intro."
- Tell them in person / call
- Consider a small gift (coffee, book, donation)

Generosity compounds. People remember who remembers them.
